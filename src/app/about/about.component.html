The Landscape Data Commons is an inter-agency monitoring data repository and portal, led by the USDA-ARS at the Jornada Experimental Range, that connects standardized monitoring data to analysis tools to support land management and research. The Landscape Data Commons aggregates core methods data collected across agencies and monitoring programs (e.g., AIM, NRI, the National Wind Erosion Research Network, smaller research and monitoring efforts). With these aggregated data, the Landscape Data Commons supports natural resource management, modeling, and research. The Landscape Data Commons provides consistent indicator calculations and distributions of indicators by classification unit (e.g., ecological site), connects these data to wind and water erosion models (e.g., AERO and RHEM), remote sensing models (e.g., RAP), and decision support tools (e.g., the Land Treatment Exploration Tool, the Ecological Dynamics Interpretive Tool).

To get involved, contact Sarah McCord (sarah.mccord@usda.gov) or Nicholas Webb (nwebb@nmsu.edu).

Partner Programs

The Landscape Data Commons is a partnership of a diversity of monitoring programs.

Bureau of Land Management Assessment, Inventory, and Monitoring Program

The Assessment, Inventory, and Monitoring (AIM) provides a framework for the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) to inventory and quantitatively assess the condition and trend of natural resources on the nation's federal public lands. AIM data are collected through a combination of probabilistic surveys and target sampling at national, regional, and project scales. Terrestrial AIM data include line-point intercept, gap intercept, vegetation height, and species inventory. Many plots also contain soil stability values and 70 cm soil pits. (https://aim.landscapetoolbox.org)

National Wind Erosion Research Network

The National Wind Erosion Research Network is a multi-partner effort, led by the USDA-ARS, with three general aims: (1) provide data to support understanding of wind erosion rates across land use and land cover types under different management systems, (2) support the development of an all-lands wind erosion model and decision support tools for managing wind erosion and air quality, and (3) facilitate collaboration across agencies and the research community in addressing these challenges. The network uses standardized sensors to measure aeolian sediment transport rates, dust deposition, dust concentrations and dust emission, and collects line-point intercept, vegetation height and gap intercept methods for soil surface and vegetation properties. Vegetation monitoring methods and models used by the network are consistent with those used by BLM AIM and NRCS NRI (https://winderosionnetwork.org).

Natural Resources Conservation Service National Resources Inventory

The National Resources Inventory (NRI) Grazinglands On-site program is a Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS) survey which collects core indicators on private, state, and tribal pasture and rangelands. The NRI survey collects line-point intercept, vegetation height, gap intercept, soil stability, and annual production methods in addition to qualitative assessments on the attributes of rangeland health and management concerns (https://grazingland.cssm.iastate.edu/).
